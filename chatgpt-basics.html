The Importance of Well-Structured Prompts

How to design better prompts and questions to get high-quality, trustworthy answers from ChatGPT.

üí° What is a "Prompt"?

A "prompt" is simply the question or instruction you give to ChatGPT. Think of it like giving directions to a very knowledgeable but literal person who only knows what you tell them.

Good prompts are: Specific, detailed, and include context about what you're trying to achieve.

Bad prompts are: Vague, too broad, or don't provide enough information for ChatGPT to give you a useful answer.

Good Prompt Design

  • Be specific and detailed ‚Äì Include context, requirements, and constraints
  • Ask for step-by-step explanations ‚Äì Request structured responses with clear sections
  • Specify the format you want ‚Äì Ask for lists, tables, code, or specific structures
  • Include examples ‚Äì Show what kind of response you're looking for
  • Ask for verification ‚Äì Request sources or ask it to explain its reasoning

Avoiding Poor Prompts

  • Vague questions ‚Äì "Tell me about technology" is too broad
  • Leading questions ‚Äì Don't suggest the answer you want
  • Complex multi-part questions ‚Äì Break them down into smaller parts
  • Assuming current knowledge ‚Äì Remember the training cutoff
  • Not specifying context ‚Äì Provide relevant background information

Example: Good vs Poor Prompts

Poor: "How do I cook chicken?"

Better: "I'm a beginner cook with a gas stove and need to make dinner for 4 people. What's the safest way to cook chicken breasts so they're not dry? I have basic kitchen equipment and about 30 minutes. Please give me step-by-step instructions."

Poor: "What should I do about my car?"

Better: "My 2018 Honda Civic is making a rattling noise when I accelerate, and the check engine light came on yesterday. I'm not very car-savvy and need to know if this is urgent or if I can wait until next week to get it checked. What are the most likely causes and what should I do first?"

Glossary of ChatGPT Terms

Key terms to understand when working with ChatGPT

Training Cutoff

The date when the AI model's training data ends. ChatGPT-3.5's cutoff is before January 2022, meaning it has no knowledge of events or information after that date.

Hallucination

When an AI generates information that sounds plausible but is actually incorrect or made up. Common when the AI doesn't have current information.

Web Browsing

A ChatGPT-4 feature that allows the AI to search the internet and access current information, overcoming the training cutoff limitation.

Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing effective prompts and questions to get better, more reliable responses from AI language models.

Large Language Model (LLM)

An AI system trained on massive amounts of text data to understand and generate human language. ChatGPT is an example of an LLM.
